# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Self-made, already-successful men (mostly Mumbai and Indian metros) who have money but carry a
quiet, unspoken insecurity: *"Do I belong in these rooms? Am I dressed correctly? Am I trying too
hard?"* They are not shopping for clothes; they are looking for someone who understands how to
carry success gracefully. Context: browsing privately, often late, deciding whether this house is
worth a visit. The job to be done is emotional, not transactional: to feel *"I've found someone who
understands"* and, by the end, to want to visit the atelier.

## Product Purpose

CEO Rules is a 145-year-old bespoke tailoring house (House of Haji Soomar, est. 1881, Bandra
Mumbai). This website is not an e-commerce store or a tailoring site: it is a digital flagship that
translates a *philosophy* into an experience. It sells a feeling: **walking into a room and knowing
you belong there.** Success = the founder looks at it and says *"This feels like us,"* and the
visitor leaves with a stronger emotional connection than they arrived with, wanting to visit.

Core brand sentence every screen reinforces:
> CEO Rules is a house that teaches self-made men the quiet language of confidence through timeless
> craftsmanship.

Emotional arc the site must produce, never resetting it:
**Curiosity → Respect → Trust → Connection → Aspiration → Action.**

## Brand Personality

Three words: **Mentor. Restraint. Presence.**

- Voice is **Anis Soomar** (the founder) as a mentor and teacher, not a salesman. He teaches as
  generosity; he does not sell. The site inherits that: it guides, never pitches.
- Warm, human, and Mumbai-grounded, not pretending to be European. Power delivered with warmth;
  authority delivered through generosity.
- Confident and quiet: luxury whispers, never shouts. Emotion before information. Every section
  leaves the visitor slightly wiser than before.
- Holds its productive contradictions on purpose (heritage + modern ambition, luxury +
  approachability, education + exclusivity, Mumbai + European tailoring). Never flatten them.

## Anti-references

- **Not** an e-commerce / Shopify / catalogue site. No product grids, prices, "add to cart," "shop."
- **Not** the generic premium-tailor template: hero of a man in a suit, About Us, heritage timeline,
  products, testimonials, contact. Technically correct, completely forgettable.
- **Not** cold, minimal-for-minimalism's-sake European luxury imitation. Not corporate,
  sales-driven, trendy, flashy, overly masculine, intimidating, or trying-too-hard-to-look-luxurious.
- No stock imagery, no AI-generated business people. Authentic photography only.
- Reference houses to study for *pacing, restraint, storytelling* (not to copy): Loro Piana,
  Brunello Cucinelli, Zegna, Tom Ford, Rolls-Royce, Aman Resorts, Apple.

## Design Principles

1. **Emotion before information.** People remember feelings, atmosphere, and stories, not sections,
   animations, or paragraphs. Create emotion before understanding, curiosity before information.
2. **Guide, never sell.** Answer "what would Anis naturally say if you walked into the store?"
   Every section teaches something true. The visit should feel inevitable, not solicited.
3. **Luxury means restraint.** Do not over-design, over-animate, or over-write. Leave breathing
   room. Confidence, not decoration. If something can't justify why it exists, remove it.
4. **Heritage is a plot twist, not a headline.** Earn emotional investment first; reveal 1881 / 145
   years later, so the number suddenly means something.
5. **The founder is the soul, woven throughout.** Anis is not an "About" section; he is the
   emotional center, present naturally across the experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA: text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (≥ 3:1 for large display type), visible focus states,
  full keyboard operability, semantic landmarks.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: motion is atmospheric, never load-bearing; provide calm static
  fallbacks. No motion that reduces usability.
- Responsive and legible on mobile first (most Indian traffic is mobile); never sacrifice
  readability for effect.
